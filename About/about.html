History

Founding of AIEE (1884)

The IEEE story begins in 1884 with the founding of the American Institute of Electrical Engineers (AIEE), with notable members like Thomas Edison and Alexander Graham Bell. The AIEE focused on the rapidly growing field of electrical power systems and helped set technical standards.

Formation of IRE (1912)

The Institute of Radio Engineers (IRE) was established in 1912, focusing on radio technology and telecommunications. The IRE attracted engineers from these fields, marking a significant expansion in technology focus.

Merger of AIEE and IRE (1963)

In 1963, the AIEE and IRE merged to form the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), creating a unified organization to support electrical, electronics, and computing engineering disciplines.

Expanding Fields and Global Influence

Since its formation, IEEE has expanded to include fields like computing, aerospace, biomedical engineering, and robotics, growing to become a global organization with members in over 160 countries.

Today

Today, IEEE is a leader in technological innovation, standards development, and research publications, advancing technology for the benefit of humanity and shaping the future of engineering across various industries.
